Record High Temperatures in Arctic

Arctic region experiences unprecedented heatwaves, impacting wildlife and ecosystems. The highest temperature ever recorded on the most northern side of the planet has been officially confirmed by the WMO on 20 June 2020 in the Siberian town of Verkhoyansk: 38°C. Temperatures in the Arctic are raising at a rate more than twice the global average. This is causing record numbers of "zombie fires" because of the burning of carbon-rich peat, the thawing of permafrost and the breaking up of Arctic's thickest ice. The highest temperature ever recorded in the Arctic was 38°C (100.4°F) in the Russian town of Verkhoyansk on June 20, 2020. The temperature was recorded during a prolonged Siberian heatwave, and the town is located about 115 kilometers north of the Arctic Circle. The World Meteorological Organization (WMO) added a new climate category to its international archive to recognize the record.
